How the score works

Each neighborhood is scored 1–10 on fifteen criteria; the overall score is a weighted average. The score wheel on each card draws each criterion as a wedge (length = score), labeled with three-letter codes — hover any wedge or label for its meaning and score — in five color families: education, lifestyle, land & mountains, practical, safety & risk. Zero-weighted wedges fade.

score = Σ(criterion × weight) ÷ Σ(weights) → 0.00–10.00

Weight 0 removes a criterion. Region ranks are live everywhere, including the map. Commute is computed from real distance to your chosen anchor (Downtown, DTC, or Boulder); airport is distance to DIA. Hazard risk & insurability blends wildfire exposure (WUI), hail, and the post-Marshall insurance market — under Colorado’s HB25-1182 (effective July 2026), any buyer can demand a property’s insurer wildfire score and formally appeal it. Safety reflects relative property/violent crime patterns; both are editorial 1–10 reads, not actuarial ratings. Expand any area to see its zoned schools by address — the real attendance-boundary assignment from the NCES School Attendance Boundary Survey (2015–16 vintage; choice districts and new-growth areas may differ, so verify with the district) — shown alongside the proximity-based "ideal path" of best-ranked nearby schools.

The map

  • Solid boundaries — real polygons from Denver's official statistical-neighborhood open data.
  • Dashed boundaries — approximate geometric shapes for towns outside Denver city limits (Louisville, Golden, etc.); swap in Census place polygons later for full accuracy.
  • Pins — exact school locations; the pin tip is the spot, the head color is the type (red private, blue public, green charter/magnet), the number is composite rank.
  • Everything — boundaries, roads, water, pins — runs through one lat/lng projection, so positions are mutually true.

School list ranking

Fixed editorial composite from Niche/US News standings, program distinctiveness, and this research. Filter by type (public includes charters/magnets) and level — including early childhood, ranked by an editorial blend of accreditation (NAEYC), Colorado Shines ratings, and program influence, since no Niche/US News composite exists for preschools. Schools spanning levels appear in every band they serve. Vitals and diversity figures shown without a ~ are actuals from federal data: NCES CCD 2022–23 for public schools (enrollment, student:teacher, students of color, free/reduced lunch) and the NCES Private School Universe Survey 2021–22 for most privates (no FRL program exists at private schools). Figures with a ~ are editorial estimates for schools absent from those surveys; early-childhood programs rarely publish demographics — "not published" means exactly that, not zero. ECE waitlist reads are coarse editorial bands (no published source for wait times exists) — treat them as reputation, verify with the school, and assume infant rooms fill first everywhere. Rank shown is position within the current view. Access shows how a family actually gets a seat (Boundary, Lottery, Audition, Gifted ID, Tuition). Each card carries a five-wedge program wheel — Experiential, Arts, Athletics, STEM, Small & personal — independent of the composite rank; hover a wedge for its meaning. Four of the five (Experiential, Arts, Athletics, STEM) are editorial 1–10 reads of program emphasis — informed judgment about each school's stated model, not measured outcomes; Small & personal is computed from the real student:teacher ratio. A candid caveat on bias: this list carries roughly equal public and private entries, but the composite blends Niche/US News rankings (which correlate with household affluence) with editorial judgment, and private schools genuinely run far smaller classes — so read the wheels as emphasis and fit, not verdicts, and weight the dimensions yourself with "Customize school weights."
