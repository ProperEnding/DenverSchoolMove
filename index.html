How the score works

Each neighborhood is scored 1–10 on fourteen criteria; the overall score is a weighted average. The card wheel draws each criterion as a wedge (length = score) in five color families: education, lifestyle, land & mountains, practical, safety & risk. Zero-weighted wedges fade.

score = Σ(criterion × weight) ÷ Σ(weights) → 0.00–10.00

Weight 0 removes a criterion. Region ranks are live everywhere, including the map. Commute is computed from real distance to your chosen anchor (Downtown, DTC, or Boulder); airport is distance to DIA. Hazard risk & insurability blends wildfire exposure (WUI), hail, and the post-Marshall insurance market — under Colorado’s HB25-1182 (effective July 2026), any buyer can demand a property’s insurer wildfire score and formally appeal it. Safety reflects relative property/violent crime patterns; both are editorial 1–10 reads, not actuarial ratings.

The map

  • Solid boundaries — real polygons from Denver's official statistical-neighborhood open data.
  • Dashed boundaries — approximate geometric shapes for towns outside Denver city limits (Louisville, Golden, etc.); swap in Census place polygons later for full accuracy.
  • Pins — exact school locations; the pin tip is the spot, the head color is the type (red private, blue public, green charter/magnet), the number is composite rank.
  • Everything — boundaries, roads, water, pins — runs through one lat/lng projection, so positions are mutually true.
